    About Me

    I received my B.S. degree in Biomedical and Electrical Engineering from Duke University, Durham, NC, in 2000. I was a James B. Duke graduate fellow and received my Ph.D. degree in Biomedical Engineering from Duke University in 2005 and my M.D. degree from the Duke University School of Medicine in 2007. I am currently an Assistant Research Professor in Biomedical Engineering and Anesthesiology at Duke University. My research interests include acoustic radiation force shear wave elasticity imaging, ultrasonic imaging, and finite element analysis of soft tissue response to acoustic radiation force excitation. I currently teach Introduction to Medical Instrumentation (BME354L) and Medical Instrumentation (BME464L).
